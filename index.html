Kolli Hills.

The 'Mountain of Death', Reborn as a Sanctuary.

Ascend 1,300 meters (4,265 ft) into a realm guarded by the deity Kollipavai. Historically avoided for its mysterious allure, today it stands as an untouched paradise of 14 Nadus (villages) in the Eastern Ghats, where time slows and nature speaks.

Arapaleeswarar

In a world of constant noise, we found something incredibly rare: Silence. The Arapaleeswarar Temple isn't just a structure; it's a testament to permanence. Deeply rooted in the 1st century AD, it stands as a sentinel of the hills.

Dedicated to Lord Shiva and Goddess Aram Valartha Nayagi, this masterpiece was patronized by the Sangam-age King Valvil Ori.

The Legend: A plow accidentally injured a Shiva Lingam, which started to bleed. The scar is said to be visible on the deity's head even today.

The Mystery: It is widely believed that a secret underground path connects this sanctum directly to the Shiva temple in Rasipuram, kilometers away.

Siddhar Caves

This isn't just a hike; it's a pilgrimage into the earth. Sages like Pampatti Siddhar and the father of Siddha medicine, Agasthiar, chose these narrow crevices for their deep meditation.

The entrance to the caves is a test of humility—often so narrow that it admits only one person at a time, forcing you to crawl into the womb of the earth. Surrounded by groves of rare medicinal herbs, the air itself is believed to be curative.

Trek Duration: 30-60 minutes. Proceed with reverence.

Agaya Gangai

The 'Ganges of the Sky'.

Fed by the jungle stream Panchanathi (River of Five), this 300-foot cascade is a raw display of nature's power. We stripped away the unnecessary, leaving only the water, the gravity, and the thunder.

The Descent: Accessible only by a pilgrimage of 1,196 steep steps. It takes 20 mins to descend, but the climb back is a test of will.

The Water: Cascading through rich herbal forests, the water is believed to possess medicinal properties. A dip here is said to wash away more than just dust.

Best Time: Post-monsoon (Oct-Jan). Caution: Rocks can be slippery.

TAMPCOL Farm

Healing from the earth.

Established in 1983, this State Government run sanctuary is a living library of Siddha, Ayurveda, and Unani medicine. It is not just a farm; it is a preservation of ancient knowledge.

The Experience: Walk among aromatic groves and purchase authentic products produced on-site:
• Eucalyptus & Lemongrass Oil • Herbal Powders & Tonics
Open daily: 8 AM to 7 PM.

Botanical Garden

Located in Semmedu, this is where wild nature meets curated beauty. Featuring a magnificent Rose Garden with hybrid varieties that bloom in colors you didn't know existed.

Must See: The Viewpoint Telescope, offering a closer look at the distant plains, and the eco-friendly cottages that blend into the landscape.
Open: 8 AM - 8 PM.

Boat House

A pristine artificial lake bordered by gentle hillocks. The water here is a mirror for the sky.

The Highlight: A small, lush island sits in the center of the lake, accessible only by boat. It's a park within a lake within a hill station.

Activities: Pedal & Row Boating. Timings: 6 AM - 6 PM.

Selur & Seekuparai

Standing at one of the highest altitudes, this spot is often called the Rock Pillar.

The Phenomenon: Here, the clouds don't just float above you; they pass through you. On a clear day, witness the sheer scale of the Eastern Ghats and the mosaic of coffee and banana plantations below.
Legend: King Pari gave his chariot to a jasmine creeper here.

Designed in California. Experienced in Tamil Nadu.

Elevation: 1,300m | Location: Namakkal District | Nearest City: Namakkal (~55km)

Best Time to Visit: All year round
