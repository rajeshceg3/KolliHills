Arapaleeswarar

In a world of constant noise, we found something incredibly rare. Silence. The Arapaleeswarar Temple isn't just a structure; it's a testament to permanence. Deeply rooted in the 1st century AD, it stands as a sentinel of the hills.

Dedicated to Lord Shiva and Goddess Aram Valartha Nayagi, this masterpiece was patronized by the Sangam-age King Valvil Ori, a legendary archer. The Dravidian architecture bears inscriptions from the Chola period, standing as a silent witness to centuries of devotion.

The temple is the epicenter of the Aadi Perukku festival and is believed to have a secret underground path connecting to the Shiva temple in Rasipuram.

Siddhar Caves

An age-old home for ascetics. Legend whispers that sages like Pampatti Siddhar and Agasthiar meditated here, mastering the ancient healing arts. The silence here is not empty; it is full of history and the scent of medicinal herbs.

The caves, including the Agasthiar Kugai, are often so narrow that they admit only one person at a time. Surrounded by groves of rare herbs, the air itself feels curative.
Trek Duration: Approx. 30-60 minutes.

Agaya Gangai

The Ganges of the Sky.

Fed by the Panchanathi river, this 300-foot cascade is a raw display of nature's power. We stripped away the unnecessary, leaving only the water, the gravity, and the sound.

The Descent: Accessible only by a pilgrimage of 1,050 steep steps. A challenge for the body, a reward for the spirit.

The Water: Cascading through rich herbal forests, the water is believed to possess medicinal properties before it crashes into the sacred pool below.

TAMPCOL Farm

Healing from the earth.

Run by the Tamil Nadu Medical Plant Farms & Herbal Medicine Corporation. This sprawling estate is a living library of Siddha, Ayurveda, and Unani medicine.

Visitors can explore varieties of medicinal plants and purchase authentic herbal products like oils and powders produced right here. Open daily from 8 AM to 7 PM.

Botanical Garden

Ideally located in Semmedu. A vibrant contrast to the rugged hills, featuring a magnificent Rose Garden with hybrid varieties, a fun-filled Children's Park, and eco-friendly cottages.

Open: 8 AM - 8 PM.

Selur Point

One of the highest points in the hills, also known as the Rock Pillar. Legend has it that King Pari gave his chariot to a jasmine creeper here. The clouds don't just float above you; they pass through you. A place to witness the sheer scale of the Eastern Ghats and the lush coffee and banana plantations below.
